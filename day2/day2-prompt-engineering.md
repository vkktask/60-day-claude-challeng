# Day 2: What Is Prompt Engineering
*ABTalks 60 Days Claude Challenge*

## Section 1: Explanation

**What Prompt Engineering is**
Prompt engineering is the practice of writing clear, structured instructions so an AI model gives you the response you actually want. It is less about tricking the AI and more about communicating like you would with a very literal, very capable new employee: the more precise your request, the better the output.

**Why it matters when using AI tools like Claude**
Claude, and any large language model, does not read your mind - it responds to exactly what you type. A vague prompt gets a vague, generic answer. A well-engineered prompt gives Claude the context, format, and constraints it needs to produce something useful on the first try, saving you rounds of back-and-forth editing.

**Three practical benefits of writing better prompts**
1. Saves time - fewer follow-up corrections because the first response is closer to what you need.
2. Improves accuracy - specific instructions reduce ambiguity, so the AI is less likely to guess wrong.
3. Unlocks better formatting and structure - you can ask for the exact output shape you need (tables, code, step-by-step guides) instead of reformatting the AI's answer yourself.

## Section 2: Weak vs Improved Prompt Example

**Weak prompt:** "Explain AI to me."
Result: A generic, unfocused answer that could be too basic or too technical, with no clear structure.

**Improved prompt:** "You are an AI educator teaching complete beginners with no technical background. Explain what Artificial Intelligence is in 3 short paragraphs, using one real-world analogy, and end with 2 practical examples of AI in everyday apps."
Result: A focused, appropriately-leveled explanation with a consistent structure, an analogy for intuition, and concrete examples - ready to use with no editing.
