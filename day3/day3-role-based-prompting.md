# Day 3: Role-Based Prompting
*ABTalks 60 Days Claude Challenge*

## Section 1: Explanation

**What Role-Based Prompting is**
Role-based prompting means telling the AI who to be before asking your question - for example, You are a senior product manager or You are an experienced career coach. Instead of getting a generic answer, you're setting the lens the AI uses to think about your request: what it prioritizes, what vocabulary it uses, and what kind of advice it gives.

**Why it matters when using AI tools like Claude**
Claude can produce very different quality answers to the exact same question depending on the context you give it. Without a role, it defaults to a generalist tone. With a role, it draws on the patterns, priorities, and language specific to that expertise - much closer to what an actual expert would say.

**How assigning a role changes the quality of AI responses**
A role narrows the AI's frame of reference. It signals what to prioritize (a lawyer prioritizes risk, a marketer prioritizes persuasion), what depth of detail is appropriate, and what tone fits the audience. This alone can turn a shallow, generic answer into one that reads like it came from a specialist.

**Example without a role prompt:** "How should I price my product?"
Result: A generic list of common pricing strategies with no context on your business, audience, or constraints.

**Example with a role prompt:** "You are an experienced SaaS pricing consultant who has helped 50+ early-stage startups. A founder is launching a project management tool for small teams. Recommend a pricing strategy, explain the reasoning, and flag one common mistake to avoid."
Result: A focused, context-aware recommendation with reasoning grounded in SaaS pricing patterns and a specific pitfall - much closer to real consulting advice.

**Three practical benefits of Role-Based Prompting**
1. Sharper expertise - answers draw on the vocabulary, priorities, and reasoning patterns of a specific field instead of generic knowledge.
2. Consistent tone and depth - you control whether the response reads like a beginner's guide or an expert briefing.
3. Faster iteration - you can quickly compare perspectives on the same problem by swapping roles (e.g., now answer as a skeptical investor).

## Section 2: Persona Comparison

Developer: technical accuracy, code-level detail, focus on implementation trade-offs.
Product Manager: prioritization, user impact, roadmap and trade-off framing.
HR Manager: people/process sensitivity, policy language, compliance awareness.
Founder: big-picture strategy, resourcefulness, risk/reward framing.
Marketer: persuasive language, audience targeting, brand voice.
